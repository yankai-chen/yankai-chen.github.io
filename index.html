Yankai Chen

陈焱凯

Postdoctoral Associate
Cornell University

I am a postdoctoral associate at Cornell University, working on topics of AI for scientific and healthcare problems, adviced by Prof. Chang Su and Prof. Yunyu Xiao. Recently, I am visiting Prof. Philip S. Yu at UIC. I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science and Engineering from The Chinese University of Hong Kong in 2023, supervised by Prof. Irwin King. Prior to that, I completed my M.S. degree at The University of Hong Kong in 2018, working with Prof. Reynold Cheng, and received my B.S. degree from Nanjing University in 2016, mentored by Prof. Qizhi Liu.

My research interest is Artificial Intelligence for Multimodal Information Retrieval, Processing, and Adaptation with Certainty-awareness. My recent topics include but are not limited to:

  • Large language model adaptation problems, e.g., parameter-efficient fine-tuning.
  • Multimodal information retrieval, e.g., text-video retrieval, genomic and whole slide image analysis.
  • AI for scientific problems, e.g., molecular representation learning, drug target affinity prediction.
  • Large-scale graph data mining and learning problems, e.g., community retrieval, graph neural networks, self-supervised graph learning.
  • Information retrieval optimization problems, e.g., knowledge graph, low-bit quantization, learning to hash.
  • Information retrieval applications, e.g., Search and recommender systems.
  • AI4DB problems, e.g., ANN search and optimization for vector database, motif prediction.

Feel free to contact me if you are seeking any form of collaboration.
